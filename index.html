Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal in Agra, India, is a breathtaking white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century as a tribute to his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. This UNESCO World Heritage site is renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and serene gardens, making it a symbol of eternal love and one of the world's most iconic monuments.

Red Fort

The Red Fort, or Lal Qila, in Delhi, is a magnificent 17th-century fortress built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. Constructed from red sandstone, this UNESCO World Heritage site served as the main residence of Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. It features stunning architecture, including the Diwan-i-Aam and Diwan-i-Khas, symbolizing India's rich history and cultural heritage.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, or Sri Harmandir Sahib, located in Amritsar, Punjab, is the holiest shrine in Sikhism. Built in the 16th century by Guru Arjan Dev, it features a stunning golden facade and is surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar, a sacred pool. The temple symbolizes inclusivity and unity, attracting millions of devotees and visitors from around the world for spiritual solace monuments.
